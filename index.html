What is the Kilmann Model?

The Thomas-Kilmann Model assess an individual behaviour in conflict situations. This means that this model helps us understand how people typically respond when facing disagreements or challenges . This focuses on two basic dimensions:

Assertiveness: Refers to the extent to which a person actively pursues their own concerns and gaols in a conflict situation. It's about how they push for their own way rather than thinking of others


Cooperativeness: Refers to the extent which a person seeks to satisfy the other persons concerns and goals over their own goals.


Based on these 2 basic dimensions, it introduces us to 5 conflict modes and how they can adapt their behaviour to different situations. These modes are:

  1. Competing

  2. Collaborating

  3. Avoiding

  4. Accommodating

  5. Compromising

The below figure will give you a visual of where each conflict mode lies in between the 2 basic dimensions of assertiveness and cooperativeness. These conflict styles can also improve communication and problem solving skills.
